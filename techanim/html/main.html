4 Feb 3. More Kinematics So we did some reviews on different Jacobian methods we covered last lecture and talked about newer inverse kinematics and the trend in the technology. We looked at some interesting application of IK and some things really got my attention. The sega example (The one with constraints) was pretty impressive for the time. I was wondering how maya does it now or is the sega one a completely different approach? When we were talking about the parallelized ik computation I was thinking of my 462 IK which was pretty slow. Maybe some adaptaion of the technique would have improved the performance. Lastly the FABRIC paper was impressive too in a sense that you can do so much stuff with just IK. I usually use FK for the upper body and IK for the foot and thought it would be impossible to replicate movements with just IK, but seems like it's not true.
6 Feb 4. Skinning Today we talked about skinning, which was a topic I was looking forward to have a class about. I was always wondering what the different options in maya was (I guess I was not that curious about them to actually look up) and wanted to learn how skinning is done. Also skinnning is a extremely time consuming and tidious process so I was exited to learn how to make it better. We talked about linear blend skinning and dual quaternian skinning, but I think the physics simulation integration was something I want to look more into if I have more time. The concept of not having to keyframe every secondary motion was very tempting. Also I wonder how heatmap skinning works? When I had to skin in maya I had an advice that starting with heatmap skinning will give better results.
11 Feb Paper presentation 1 There were three paper presentations about chracter rigging. Two of them were paper I was also interested in, so it was nice to actually learn what the papers were about. The Toonsynth one was about generating stylized 2d animation with existing sequence by decomposing the character sprite into different body parts like a puppet. I thought the paper was about generating animation from a character illustration, but apparently it requires a little more artist interaction. The empirical jaw rig was another interesting paper. The disney crews came up with a jaw rig that takes care of specific movement of actual jaw movement by collecting a lot of data. Seems like the trend of animation related research is really going to the data driven path. I didn't really get the math part but I think I got the gist of it like mapping the 3d space where the tip of the jaw can be to parameter space so that the artists can ineract with the rig.. (If I got it wrong please tell me). The natural language scene creation was cool too. It used some mixture of natural language processor and a tree structure to compose a scene to generate plausible scene from sentences. I thought this one would be really usefull for the artists since it is pretty hard to randomly come up with a scene. However, one thing that I was concerened is that since the neural network is trained with limited dataset, won't there be not enough noise to the composition? aka, won't the result would be repetitive or in similar composision? Well I think at that point the artists should fidget around with the created scene to get diversity.

Also the mini projects were out but I really don't think I can start them before spring break..
